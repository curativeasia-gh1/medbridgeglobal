Market: Singapore

HSA Medical Device Registration Singapore — Class A, B, C and D Guide

A practical guide for manufacturers planning to register medical devices with Singapore's Health Sciences Authority (HSA), covering classification, registration pathways, documentation and post-market obligations.

Regulatory Framework

Medical devices in Singapore are regulated by the Health Sciences Authority (HSA) under the Health Products Act (HPA). The HSA's Medical Devices Branch is responsible for product registration, dealer licensing, product vigilance and post-market safety surveillance.

Singapore's framework is one of the most developed in Southeast Asia and is internationally recognised. The HSA participates actively in ASEAN harmonisation initiatives and maintains mutual recognition arrangements with several international regulatory bodies. Marketing authorisations from recognised jurisdictions (US FDA, CE, TGA Australia and others) can support or streamline HSA registration through the Abridged or Verification application pathways.

Device Classification

Singapore uses a four-class risk-based classification aligned with ASEAN AMDD guidelines:

Class Risk Level Examples
Class A Low Bandages, examination gloves, hospital beds
Class B Low–Moderate Hypodermic needles, ultrasound equipment, contact lenses
Class C Moderate–High Haemodialysis machines, ventilators, bone fixation plates
Class D High Heart valves, IVDs for blood safety, neural stimulators

Registration Pathways

HSA offers several registration pathways depending on the supporting evidence available:

  • Full Dossier: Applicable to all classes. Requires complete technical documentation submitted directly to HSA.
  • Abridged: For devices already approved by a recognised authority (US FDA, CE, TGA, Health Canada, MHRA, PMDA). Reduces the local documentation burden.
  • Verification: For devices approved in two or more recognised jurisdictions. Fastest available pathway for eligible products.
  • In-Vitro Diagnostic (IVD) specific pathways: Applied to IVD devices based on their intended use and risk classification.

Dealer Licensing and Product Registrant

To place medical devices on the Singapore market, the importer or local responsible entity must hold a valid Dealer's Licence from the HSA. The Product Registrant — the entity that holds the product registration — must be a Singapore-registered entity.

Foreign manufacturers can appoint an independent Product Registrant separate from their commercial distributor. This structure protects the manufacturer's registration continuity if commercial arrangements change and allows the manufacturer to retain ownership of the regulatory investment.

Key Documentation

  • Device description and intended use
  • Classification justification
  • Quality management system evidence (ISO 13485 certificate or equivalent)
  • Technical documentation and test reports
  • Clinical evaluation report (class-dependent)
  • Labelling in English
  • Overseas marketing approvals (if applicable pathway)
  • Manufacturer authorisation letter to the Product Registrant
  • Declaration of Conformity

Indicative Timelines

Class Full Dossier Abridged / Verification
Class A 3–6 months 2–4 months
Class B 6–12 months 4–8 months
Class C 12–18 months 6–12 months
Class D 15–24 months 9–15 months

Timelines are indicative only. Actual processing is controlled by the HSA and may vary. Incomplete submissions, queries from the HSA and backlog may extend timelines.

Post-Registration Obligations

  • Product registration renewal (typically every five years)
  • Dealer licence renewal (annually)
  • Change notifications for product, labelling or manufacturing changes
  • Adverse event reporting to HSA Medical Device Vigilance System
  • Field safety corrective action and recall coordination
  • Post-market surveillance documentation maintenance

Frequently Asked Questions

What authority regulates medical devices in Singapore?

Medical devices in Singapore are regulated by the Health Sciences Authority (HSA) under the Health Products Act. The HSA's Medical Devices Branch administers product registration, dealer licensing and post-market surveillance.

Can a manufacturer hold their own HSA registration independently of the distributor?

Yes. A manufacturer can appoint an independent Product Registrant — separate from the commercial distributor — to hold the HSA registration. This protects regulatory continuity if the commercial distribution arrangement changes. Medbridge can act as an independent Product Registrant where product scope and local regulations permit.

Does CE marking help with HSA registration?

Yes. CE marking from a Notified Body is one of the recognised overseas approvals that qualifies a device for the Abridged or Verification pathways, which typically have shorter assessment timelines than the Full Dossier pathway. HSA maintains an updated list of recognised jurisdictions and qualification criteria.
