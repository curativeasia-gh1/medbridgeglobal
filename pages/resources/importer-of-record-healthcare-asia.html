What is Importer of Record in Healthcare — Asia Market Entry Guide

A practical guide for medical device and healthcare manufacturers on Importer of Record (IOR) services — what it means, when you need it and how it works across Hong Kong, Singapore, Malaysia and Thailand.

What is an Importer of Record?

An Importer of Record (IOR) is the legally responsible entity for importing goods into a specific country. The IOR is named on the import declaration, assumes legal responsibility for customs compliance, pays applicable duties and taxes, and ensures the importation meets local regulatory requirements.

For medical devices and healthcare products, being the Importer of Record carries additional obligations beyond standard customs compliance. The IOR must typically hold relevant licences from the health authority of the destination market and maintain documentation tracing the product from import through to delivery.

Why Manufacturers Use an IOR Service

Most medical device manufacturers entering Asia for the first time do not want to — or are not ready to — establish their own legal entity in every target market. Setting up subsidiaries is slow, expensive and adds ongoing compliance obligations in each country.

An IOR service allows a manufacturer to import products legally into a market without establishing a local subsidiary. The IOR takes on the import responsibility, customs filings, duties and local licensing requirements on behalf of the manufacturer — enabling faster, lower-cost market entry.

IOR vs Distributor — What is the Difference?

Role Importer of Record Distributor
Primary responsibility Legal importation, customs clearance, duties, import compliance Commercial sale, marketing, customer relationships
Who can do it Licensed local entity, appointed service provider, or distributor Commercial entity with sales capability
Can be separated Yes — IOR can be independent of the distributor Yes — distributor may or may not be the IOR
Manufacturer benefit Importation continuity when distributors change Commercial reach and market development

Separating the IOR role from the commercial distributor is a strategic choice that gives manufacturers greater continuity — if a distributor relationship ends, the import flow does not have to stop.

IOR Requirements Across the Four Markets

🇭🇰 Hong Kong

Hong Kong operates as a free port with minimal import duties on most goods. Medical devices do not require product registration in Hong Kong (with some exceptions). However, certain products require notification or authorisation under the Pharmacy and Poisons Ordinance or other applicable legislation. The importer must be a locally registered entity and maintain appropriate records. Hong Kong also serves as a regional logistics hub for onward distribution into Mainland China and Southeast Asia.

🇸🇬 Singapore

In Singapore, the Importer of Record must hold a valid HSA Dealer's Licence covering importation of the relevant device class. The IOR is responsible for ensuring products entering Singapore have been registered (or are exempt from registration) with the Health Sciences Authority. Import permits may be required for certain controlled devices. The IOR must maintain documentation tracing each shipment.

🇲🇾 Malaysia

In Malaysia, the Importer of Record must hold an MDA Establishment Licence covering importation activities. The import licence is tied to the specific device classes and registration status of the products being imported. All medical devices entering Malaysia must be registered or listed with the MDA before importation. The importer must maintain product traceability and lot-level records.

🇹🇭 Thailand

In Thailand, the Importer of Record must hold a Thai FDA medical device licence and, for registered devices, an import licence per product. Imported medical devices must be registered with the Thai FDA before they can be marketed. Import permits are issued per shipment for certain device categories. The IOR is responsible for ensuring only registered and appropriately licensed products are imported.

What an IOR Service Covers

A professional IOR service for healthcare products typically includes:

  • Local importer appointment and licensing maintenance
  • Import permit applications where required
  • Shipment documentation review and verification
  • Customs clearance coordination
  • Duties and tax documentation and payment coordination
  • Import record maintenance and traceability
  • Shipment receipt, inspection and release
  • Exception and discrepancy management
  • Coordination with regulatory and logistics partners

Frequently Asked Questions

Does a medical device manufacturer need to be the Importer of Record?

No. A manufacturer does not need to establish a local entity to import their products. A qualified local entity can act as Importer of Record on behalf of the foreign manufacturer, avoiding the need to incorporate in every market.

Can the Importer of Record be different from the distributor?

Yes, and for many manufacturers this separation is deliberate. Using an independent IOR means that if a commercial distributor is changed, the import flow does not need to be interrupted and the manufacturer retains control of the importation structure.

Is the Importer of Record the same as the licence holder?

Not necessarily. The licence holder (entity that holds the product registration) and the Importer of Record can be the same entity or different entities, depending on the market and the structure chosen. Medbridge can coordinate both roles under one regional structure where permitted.
