Market: Malaysia

MDA Medical Device Registration Malaysia — Requirements and Timeline

A practical guide for manufacturers planning to register medical devices with Malaysia's Medical Device Authority (MDA), covering classification, documentation, timelines and local representation requirements.

Regulatory Framework

Medical devices in Malaysia are regulated by the Medical Device Authority (MDA) under the Medical Device Act 2012 (Act 737) and the Medical Device Regulations 2012. The MDA sits within the Ministry of Health Malaysia and is responsible for registration, licensing, surveillance and enforcement.

Malaysia's framework is broadly aligned with the Global Harmonization Task Force (GHTF) approach, using a risk-based classification system and requiring both manufacturer establishment licensing and product registration before a medical device can be marketed in Malaysia.

Device Classification

Malaysia uses a four-class risk-based system:

Class Risk Level Examples Pathway
Class A Low Bandages, tongue depressors, non-sterile gloves Listing (non-sterile, non-measuring) or Registration
Class B Low–Moderate Hypodermic needles, suction equipment, blood pressure monitors Registration
Class C Moderate–High Haemodialysis equipment, ventilators, orthopaedic implants Registration with enhanced documentation
Class D High Heart valves, absorbable sutures, IVDs for blood screening Full registration with clinical evidence

Establishment Licensing

Before a product can be registered, the manufacturer and importer must hold an Establishment Licence. Foreign manufacturers without a Malaysian entity must appoint a Local Authorised Representative (LAR) incorporated in Malaysia. The LAR applies for and holds the establishment licence on behalf of the foreign manufacturer.

The establishment licence must be renewed annually and covers the types of medical device activities the entity is permitted to perform in Malaysia. Importers and distributors also require their own establishment licences covering importation and distribution activities.

Product Registration Requirements

The standard technical documentation package for MDA registration typically includes:

  • Device description, intended purpose and indications for use
  • Risk classification justification
  • Manufacturer information and quality system evidence (ISO 13485 or equivalent)
  • Technical documentation and design dossier
  • Performance and safety testing data
  • Clinical evaluation or clinical evidence (class-dependent)
  • Labelling and instructions for use
  • Declaration of Conformity
  • Marketing authorisations from reference markets (CE, FDA, TGA or others may support the submission)
  • Manufacturer authorisation letter appointing the LAR
  • Post-market surveillance plan

Indicative Timelines

MDA registration timelines depend on device class, the completeness of the submitted dossier and MDA workload. The timelines below are indicative only — actual approval is controlled by the MDA and cannot be guaranteed.

Class Establishment Licence Registration (indicative)
Class A 2–4 months 3–6 months
Class B 2–4 months 6–9 months
Class C 2–4 months 9–15 months
Class D 2–4 months 12–18+ months

Post-Registration Obligations

Once registered, manufacturers and their LARs must maintain ongoing compliance obligations in Malaysia, including:

  • Annual establishment licence renewal
  • Product registration renewal (typically every five years)
  • Change notifications for labelling, intended use, design or manufacturing changes
  • Medical device adverse event reporting to the MDA
  • Field safety corrective action coordination
  • Post-market surveillance documentation
  • Maintenance of traceability records

Frequently Asked Questions

What authority regulates medical devices in Malaysia?

Medical devices in Malaysia are regulated by the Medical Device Authority (MDA) under the Medical Device Act 2012 (Act 737) and Medical Device Regulations 2012.

Do all medical devices need to be registered in Malaysia?

Class A non-sterile, non-measuring devices may be listed rather than registered. Class A sterile or measuring devices, and all Class B, C and D devices must be registered with the MDA before they can be placed on the Malaysian market.

What is a Local Authorised Representative in Malaysia?

Foreign manufacturers without a registered establishment in Malaysia must appoint a Malaysia-incorporated Local Authorised Representative (LAR). The LAR acts as the local contact for the MDA, holds the establishment licence and takes on specified regulatory responsibilities. The LAR is distinct from a commercial distributor and can be appointed independently of the distributor relationship.

Can the Local Authorised Representative be independent of the distributor?

Yes. Manufacturers can appoint an independent LAR separate from their commercial distributor. This separation allows manufacturers to retain control over their regulatory registration and establishment licence even if they change distribution partners.
